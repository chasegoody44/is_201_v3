Process

Here's how we do it

  • Step 1:

    You think it!

    We start with you! Choose from our variety of options to create something different. You decide what names, translations, and designs that best suit your vision.

  • Step 2:

    We design it!

    Our designers work closely with you to bring your ideas to life. We create sketches and mockups to ensure you're completely satisfied with the final product.

  • Step 3:

    We create it!

    Once we have your approval, we move forward with creating the final product. Our team of skilled craftspeople work diligently to ensure the highest quality and attention to detail in every stitch.

  • Step 4:

    You get it!

    Finally, you receive the finished product, crafted to your specifications and ready to enjoy. You give back and become part of our story!

"Até"

Gentle Lamb

In Tagalog, Até, pronounced "ah-tay," means "sister." The phrase "maamong kordero" means "gentle lamb." This common metaphor describes someone who is calm, pure, and peaceful. The figure is placed between two bushes and forms a gyan mudra symbol with its fingers, signaling knowledge. Someone who is a "maamong kordero" has a strong sense of who they are, and that confidence shapes the way they treat others.

  • Collection: Family
  • Available in: S, M, L, XL

"Mamang"

Light of the home

In Tagalog, "Mamang," pronounced "mom-ong," is another name for "mother." The phrase "Ang ilaw ng tahanan" means "light of the home." It symbolizes the nurturing and guiding role of a mother, who brings light into the family and the home.

  • Category: Family
  • Available in: S, M, L, XL

"Kuya"

Selfless

In Tagalog, "Kuya," pronounced "koo-yah," means "older brother." The phrase "hindi makasarili" means "not self-centered." This symbolizes the protective and guiding role of an older sibling, someone who looks out for younger siblings and offers support without putting themselves first.

  • Category: Family
  • Available in: S, M, L, XL

"Papang"

The Pillar of the Home

In Tagalog, "papang," pronounced "pah-pahng," means "pillar" or "support." This design concept represents something that stands strong and provides support, much like a pillar in a building. That steady strength is "Papang."
